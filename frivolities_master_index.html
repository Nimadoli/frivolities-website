Culture plays a role in our concepts of beauty

The global averages hide significant cultural and social differences. In countries like Russia and China, overall physical attributes are seen as far more important than non-physical ones when it comes to beauty. In Russia, sexiness is strongly associated with beauty. In Saudi Arabia, not at all. You can easily stroll through the data, triggering a range of cultural stereotypes as you go. Of course, beauty is culturally constructed. It's in the eye of the beholder.

In that research we found that young women welcomed the focus on more diverse and "healthier" attitudes to women's bodies in the media and society at large. That being said, they thought such attempts were mostly swamped by the intense and relentless pressure to look a certain way, exacerbated by social media. One 20‑year‑old in the research explained it this way: My close friends will send me and share images from Instagram just saying, 'Oh my God, this person, look at this'. It's just constant and normal now and daily. The young women knew the perfect images on social media are carefully chosen, edited, filtered and adapted to produce the best possible results, that it is a skewed version of reality. However, this understanding doesn't lessen the impact those images have on their perception of their own bodies.

Note: The <object> element can also be used to contain images. Containing images within an <object> element stops the user from being able to save them. Another element that also behaves this way is the <embed> element. Both these elements cannot contain an <alt> tag.
